The comparative results show that no single model dominates every metric. Random Forest achieved the highest F1 score in this run, indicating the best balance between precision and recall for dropout detection, while LightGBM produced the strongest AUC, indicating slightly better ranking quality across decision thresholds. Logistic Regression remained competitive and is still valuable for interpretability, especially when communicating directional effects to non-technical stakeholders. In practice, the model choice should follow intervention policy: prioritize recall when the goal is to catch as many at-risk students as possible, or prioritize precision when support resources are limited and false alerts are costly.
